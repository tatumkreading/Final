Why Calculate College?

The net price college calculator allows potential students to determine an acurate amount college would
cost them. Scholarships are subtracted from amount to ensure the closest possible amount. Also supplies, dorm,
and any extra cost are added to have full amount.

BREAKING DOWN THE CALCULATIONS

Several factors go into the college calculator and it's based on how you answed the questions. These five questions are the
most important in determining the most acurate cost of college

Question #1: Cost of one year of College?

Having the cost of one year of college helps break down the cost for the amount of time you plan to attend.

Question #2: How many years do you plan on attending

Question #1 and Question #2 both depend on the other. Based on the area you plan to major in, different amount of time
is required to study. Each and every year you study at the college the more it will cost.

Question #3: How many in your household attend college

Different scholarships will give money to families and students based on how many family members go to college. Most
colleges will give families money if the family member attends the same college. Otherwise federal
scholarships will give money if any family member attends college. On average 3,000 dollars is given for every
family member in college.

Question #4: How many siblings do you have

Based on the number of siblings you have, FASFA, which is the Free Application for Federal Student Aid, will
give 1,200 dollars for every sibling you have.

Question #5: Do you plan to apply for FASFA

FASAF is a free student aid application that you can fill out to recieve finacial aid. On average, a student can
recieve 5,000 dollars for financial aid.

Extra's

On average college students will spend an extra 15,340 dollars. 11,140 dollars on renting a dorm, house, or apartment.
1,200 dollars on books and supplies needed for classes. And 3,000 on traveling and other expenses.
